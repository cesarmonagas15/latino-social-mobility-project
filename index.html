Social and Economic Mobility of Latino Youth

Bridging the achievement gap to enable social mobility among Latino youth.

What is Social Mobility?

Social mobility refers to change in a person's socio-economic situation, either in relation to their parents (inter-generational mobility) or throughout their lifetime (intra-generational mobility).

Social mobility is linked to equality of opportunity: the extent to which people have the same chances to do well in life regardless of the socio-economic background of their parents, their gender, age, sexual orientation, race, ethnicity, birthplace, or other circumstances beyond their control.

Why is this important?

In the Latino community, particularly in the US, access to equality of opportunity has not been uniform. Many immigrant families struggle to offer their children opportunties to which others are born into. Talking about ways to bridge the opportunity gap is imporant if we want to understand how to allow Latino youth to climb the socio-economic latter.

People from disadvantaged backgrounds have fewer opportunities to climb the socio-economic ladder. In European OECD countries, children with the greatest socio-economic disadvantage grow up to earn as much as 20% less as adults than those with more favourable childhoods. Across OECD countries, it takes nearly five generations for children from low-income families to approach the average income in their country. Unequal opportunities are not only a moral concern, they also undermine economic and social prosperity.
